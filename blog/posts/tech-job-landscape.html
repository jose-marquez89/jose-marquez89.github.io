The Tech Job Hiring Landscape in 2020

August 22, 2020


As a recent graduate of what some refer to as a coding bootcamp (I'm leaving comments on the Lambda School experience for another post), I have a vested interest in understanding the current state of hiring affairs. If you've stumbled across this blog post from the future, I would confidently assume your familiarity with COVID-19 and its impact on everything from mundane errands to the most salient political matters. With the restrictions imposed by the virus on social proximity, hiring for jobs that would normally require an in-person interview saw a dramatic change almost overnight. When I began my foray into Lambda School's Data Science program, I was advised to set my sights away from remote employment opportunities; with the advent of COVID-19, remote work has become the norm, albeit begrudgingly. The need for employees to stay home led to some companies carrying out layoffs, while others made plans to sell their headquarters. With these impacts in mind, I sought to investigate the current state of hiring in the tech sector, resorting to Indeed for some current insights into the tech hiring status.

Remote Work

With a quick search for your respective tech job role on Indeed, you can see a number of details about the jobs you seek, including a 'Remote' flag on each job card when the job poster wants to explicitly mark the job with that opportunity. I used that marker to help identify jobs that could offer remote positions, although it's entirely possible that some jobs could be temporarily remote for the time being. The great majority of postings (more than 80%) were not marked as remote in general, but we can see a marked shift in the adoption of the 'Remote' tag by posts as the weeks go by.
